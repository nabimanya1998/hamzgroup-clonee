]]> KAMPALA, Oct. 12 (Reuters) — The sun rose over Kololo Ceremonial Grounds as Kampala’s heartbeat pulsed anew, marked by music, dance, and enterprise. The city’s biggest annual celebration, the Kampala City Festival 2025, returned with renewed vigor, thanks to the generosity of philanthropist and real estate magnate Dr. Hamis Kiggundu, CEO of Ham Group of Companies.

Two weeks before the event, Dr. Kiggundu pledged UGX 200 million toward the festival, making his firm the largest private sponsor and reigniting enthusiasm for a celebration that symbolizes Kampala’s resilience and collective pride.

By midmorning, the city’s streets had transformed into a vibrant procession of color and rhythm. More than 4,000 Ham Group representatives, clad in festival-branded attire, paraded from Buganda Road to Kololo, accompanied by ten entertainment trucks promoting the company’s subsidiaries—from real estate and finance to philanthropy and technology. The spectacle, personally flagged off by Dr. Kiggundu, amplified this year’s theme, “Culture, Innovation, and Sustainability.”

Organized by the Kampala Capital City Authority (KCCA), the 2025 festival marked a triumphant revival after a long hiatus. Kololo buzzed with excitement as artisans displayed crafts, food vendors served local favorites, and children played in the new “Children’s Village,” a vibrant zone filled with games and art activities.

KCCA Executive Director Hajjat Sharifah Buzeki and State Minister for Kampala Hon. Kabuye Kyofatogabye lauded the collaboration between private sponsors and city authorities. “This event shows what can happen when creativity and community align,” Buzeki remarked during a site inspection.

As dusk fell, the main stage came alive with performances by top Ugandan artists including Ava Peace, Pastor Wilson Bugembe, Minister Rachael Kay, and the Watoto Children’s Choir. Prime Minister Rt. Hon. Robinah Nabbanja, attending as Guest of Honour, commended KCCA and its partners for organizing a festival that “captures Kampala’s spirit of inclusivity and joy.”

Throughout the day, Ham Group’s strong presence—from volunteer teams to event branding—was unmistakable. Social media feeds buzzed with posts tagged #HamAtKCF2025, highlighting the company’s participation and national pride under the slogan “For God and My Country.”

For Dr. Kiggundu, the festival was more than a sponsorship. It reflected his belief in public-private collaboration as a catalyst for Uganda’s growth. “Every thriving city has a private sector that drives innovation,” he said. “Our partnership with KCCA demonstrates that nation-building is a shared responsibility.”

As fireworks lit the Kampala sky, the festival concluded with a sense of unity and optimism. Citizens celebrated not only their culture but also the partnership between business and community that made it shine.

Kampala rejoiced. Ham Group inspired. And Dr. Hamis Kiggundu reminded the city that true progress is built when enterprise and society move together.

]]> The Ham Group of Companies is a Ugandan-based, privately owned multinational industrial conglomerate founded by Dr. Hamis Kiggundu. Recognized as one of East Africa’s largest business conglomerates, the group employs over 7,000 people and spans multiple sectors including real estate, agribusiness, logistics, education, finance, construction, and entertainment. Its official headquarters, famously known as The White House, is located in Uganda and serves as the nerve center of its operations.

History and Growth of the Ham Group

The Ham Group’s journey began in 2005 when Dr. Kiggundu, leveraging his early experience assisting his father, Hajji Segawa, in his textile shop, ventured into garment trading. Starting small during school holidays, he used capital from his father to establish his initial business. This foundation provided him with vital entrepreneurial skills, which he later applied to real estate and property development.

In 2009, Dr. Kiggundu established Ham Enterprises (U) Ltd, marking the group’s formal entry into commercial property development. His first major real estate project, Ham Towers, was completed in 2010 in Kampala, followed by Ham Shopping Mall within 18 months. These projects generated significant rental income and provided credibility to secure bank financing, enabling further expansion.

By 2021, the Ham Group had diversified into large-scale industrialization, particularly in agro-processing, aiming to add value to Uganda’s agricultural products. A flagship initiative is the US$156 million Integrated Agro-Processing Industrial Plant (IAIP) at Akright City, a state-of-the-art facility supporting Uganda’s agricultural sector and boosting economic development.

Subsidiaries of the Ham Group

As of June 2024, the Ham Group operates through a variety of subsidiaries, each targeting specific sectors and regions:

  • Ham Enterprises Limited: The parent company focusing on real estate and commercial property development, including Ham Towers and shopping complexes.
  • Ham Palm Villas: Premium residential real estate development along the Entebbe–Kampala Expressway.
  • Ham International: A multinational presence with operations in Manchester and other global markets.
  • Ham International Express Logistics: Logistics and transportation services based in Texas, United States, facilitating international trade.
  • Hamz Link: An information technology firm in Kampala, Uganda, offering tech solutions and digital services.
  • Ham Agro-Processing Industries: Focused on the agricultural sector, including processing and value addition for Uganda’s key crops.
  • Hana International School Limited: A premier educational institution in Nsangi, emphasizing global-standard learning.
  • Hamz International Real Estate LLC: Real estate investments and developments based in Dubai.
  • Ham Agro Bank Limited: Financial services tailored for agro-processing and rural communities, supporting farmers and businesses.
  • Ham Cinemax: Entertainment and cinema ventures enhancing leisure and recreation in Uganda.
  • Kiggs International Uganda Ltd: Diversified business interests spanning manufacturing and distribution.
  • Kiham Enterprises Ltd: Supporting the group’s operations in various industrial and commercial sectors.

Sectors of Operation

The Ham Group has strategically positioned itself across multiple sectors, strengthening its influence and market dominance:

  1. Real Estate and Property Development: From Ham Towers and Ham Palm Villas to modern shopping malls, the group has transformed Uganda’s urban landscape.
  2. Agribusiness and Agro-Processing: Through the IAIP and Ham Agro-Processing Industries, the group adds value to agricultural products, enhances food security, and promotes export-oriented growth.
  3. Logistics and Transportation: Ham International Express Logistics enables seamless trade across Uganda, East Africa, and global markets.
  4. Education: Hana International School Limited offers world-class education, contributing to skill development and human capital in Uganda.
  5. Finance and Banking: Ham Agro Bank Limited facilitates financial inclusion and provides essential support for the agribusiness sector.
  6. Construction: The group’s construction projects span commercial, residential, and industrial buildings, reflecting modern design and quality.
  7. Sports and Entertainment: Through Ham Cinemax and other leisure ventures, the group enriches Uganda’s cultural and recreational landscape.
  8. General Merchandise: The group is involved in retail and trade, ensuring the availability of quality products across the region.

Achievements and Impact

The Ham Group’s ventures have had a profound impact on Uganda’s economy, employment, and industrial growth:

  • Employing over 7,000 workers, the conglomerate contributes significantly to job creation.
  • Investments like the IAIP have enhanced Uganda’s agricultural value chain, encouraging modern farming and export-oriented production.
  • Real estate projects such as Ham Towers and Ham Palm Villas have transformed urban infrastructure, attracting businesses and investors.
  • Financial services through Ham Agro Bank support farmers and small enterprises, boosting productivity and economic resilience.
  • Education and training initiatives, particularly through Hana International School, prepare a new generation of skilled professionals.

The Ham Group of Companies continues to expand its footprint across Africa and beyond, positioning itself as a model for diversified industrial conglomerates in East Africa. By integrating innovation, industrialization, and community development, the group demonstrates how private enterprise can drive national growth and regional economic integration.

]]> In a remarkable act of generosity and vision, Dr. Hamis Kiggundu, a distinguished Ugandan author, entrepreneur, and advocate for African empowerment, recently visited Namibia with a mission to uplift the nation’s youth. Committed to fostering a brighter future for Africa, Dr. Kiggundu donated 10,000 copies of his influential books, Success and Failure Based on Reason and Reality and Reason as the World Masterpiece, to students and young leaders across Namibia. His goal? To spark a mindset shift that encourages critical thinking, purpose-driven action, and a pathway to prosperity.

A Gift of Knowledge and Transformation

Dr. Kiggundu’s donation represents more than a collection of books; it’s an investment in the minds of the next generation. Through his writing, he calls on young Africans to unlock the potential within themselves, harness the power of reason, and use available resources wisely. His books touch on subjects that resonate deeply with Africa’s youth, tackling the continent’s unique challenges and laying out a roadmap to personal and communal growth built on self-reliance and logical thinking.

Reason as the World Masterpiece: A Blueprint for Africa’s Success

In Reason as the World Masterpiece, Dr. Kiggundu delves into the role of reasoning in achieving prosperity. He emphasizes that rational decision-making and purposeful action are key to overcoming Africa’s longstanding challenges. This book explores why Africa, despite its abundant resources, has yet to achieve its full potential. Dr. Kiggundu highlights that the missing link is the application of reason—an approach that Western nations have embraced to achieve progress. His message is clear and powerful: Africa’s journey from poverty to prosperity requires a focus on reasoned, purposeful actions.

Success and Failure Based on Reason and Reality: A Guide to Financial Empowerment

In contrast, Success and Failure Based on Reason and Reality provides a practical framework for financial success. This 16-chapter book offers valuable insights on building wealth, finding a sense of purpose, and setting personal goals. Since its release in 2018, it has been a source of inspiration, winning Uganda’s Book of the Year award in the Business Motivation category. Dr. Kiggundu’s practical wisdom has made this book a national bestseller, and he hopes that Namibian youth will find similar guidance and motivation within its pages.

Making Education Accessible: Expanding the Message

Understanding the importance of accessibility, Dr. Kiggundu took the extra step to translate his books into Luganda, a widely spoken language in Uganda. By offering his books in a native language, he broadens the reach of his ideas, ensuring his transformative messages are accessible to more people. The Uganda Ministry of Education and Sports, through the National Curriculum Development Center (NCDC), has recognized the value of his books, recommending them for inclusion in the national curriculum for secondary schools and institutions of higher learning. This endorsement underlines the books’ potential to inspire Ugandan students to think critically and begin investing in their futures.

A Legacy of Giving

This latest donation in Namibia is part of Dr. Kiggundu’s broader mission to give back. Since 2018, he has donated nearly a million copies of his books to schools, libraries, and educational institutions across Uganda, offering students the tools to dream big and achieve their aspirations. His generosity has empowered countless young minds, laying the foundation for a new generation of thinkers, leaders, and innovators.

Building Africa’s Future Through Knowledge

Dr. Hamis Kiggundu’s vision transcends national borders. His donation of 10,000 books to Namibian youth is a testament to his belief in Africa’s potential. By equipping young people with the knowledge and tools to think critically and act with purpose, he is laying the groundwork for a prosperous and self-sustaining Africa. His books serve as beacons of hope and inspiration, reminding every young African that their future lies in the power of reason and determination.

In a world full of challenges, Dr. Kiggundu’s message rings clear: with reason, resources, and resilience, Africa can achieve greatness. Through every book he donates, he brings this vision one step closer to reality.
